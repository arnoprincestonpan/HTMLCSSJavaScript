Cooking at Home

Kimchi Fried Rice

Want something to fill your hungry tummy? In about 20 minutes? I have the dish for you! Kimchi Fried Rice. All you need are:

Ingredients

  • leftover rice from the fridge (alternative, rice that has been cooled down)
  • green onion
  • kimchi
  • cooking oil
  • Optional - Cooked Meat of your Choice (cook separately and mix in rice frying step)
  • Optional - Garlic (cook together with scallion.)

Steps

  1. Cut Green Onion, separate the white scallions from the greens.
  2. Cut Kimchi. Make the pieces small diced sized. About 1/2 of bowl per 1 bowl of rice.
  3. Get a flat pan, heat the cooking oil. Medium-Low.
  4. When the pan is ready, put in scallion the white part of the green onion.
  5. Now once you smell the strong smell of the onion, it's time to put everything else in.
  6. Mix thoroughly.
  7. Once you start seeing a little of browning at the bottom, turn off the heat immediately, continue mixing.
  8. Cool for a minute. Serve!

Coffee at Home

When I was working Full-Time, what I hated but loved the most was my coffee. What do I mean? The coffee at work was made with a super cheap machine. And it was rarely cleaned and I had tummy-aches all the time. But now I am at home, so I can make my own coffee. How do I do it? No, not with a machine, but with something more simple from the previous century. A Moka-Pot. A Traditional Moka-Pot was made with aluminum and they are still sold in stores and you can find them in book stores as well. However; aluminum is controvercial and high amount of aluminum contact is linked to health problems. So, I got a steel version instead. Steel Moka pots are compatiable with induction stoves as well, so if you live in a condo like me. You'd need it.

Ingredients & Items

  • Moka Pot Make Sure you can use it on your stove.
  • Coffee of your Choice
  • Water Boiler
  • Rag or Oven Gloves
  • A large spoon you drink soup with.

Steps

  1. Boil some Water in your water boiler or other pot.
  2. Set your rag on the counter.
  3. Unscrew your Moka Pot bottom. Set coffee tray aside.
  4. Place the Moka Pot Bottom on the rag. If you have oven gloves you don't really need to do this.
  5. Scoop up a spoonful of grinded coffee. Place it in the coffee tray and put it onto the Moka Pot Bottom.
  6. Using the rag to cover your palm. Carefully. Screw back the Moka Pot bottom.
  7. Put the Moka Pot on medium heat.
  8. When you hear a bubbling sound. Or water boiling loudly. Remove from heat and run cold water on the pot in the sink.
  9. Serve! Do it YOUR way.
